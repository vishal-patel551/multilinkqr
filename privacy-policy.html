Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

Introduction

MultiLinkQR ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our free QR code generator service. Please read this privacy policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access or use our service.

MultiLinkQR is designed with privacy as a core principle. We do not require you to create an account, and we do not store your links, settings, or personal information in any central database. Your data remains in your control.

Information We Do NOT Collect

To make it clear what we don't do, here's what MultiLinkQR does NOT collect:

  • No Account Information: We don't require registration, so we don't collect usernames, email addresses, passwords, or any account-related information.
  • No QR Code Content: The links, labels, titles, and settings you enter are encoded directly into the generated URL and processed entirely in your browser. We never receive, store, or have access to this information.
  • No Personal Data: We don't collect names, addresses, phone numbers, or any other personally identifiable information unless you explicitly provide it to us through our contact form.
  • No Payment Information: MultiLinkQR is completely free, so we never collect credit card numbers, billing addresses, or payment details.
  • No Tracking of QR Code Scans: We don't track when, where, or how many times your QR codes are scanned. This information is not available to us.

Information We May Collect

While we minimize data collection, we may collect the following types of information to improve our service:

1. Standard Web Analytics

We use Google Analytics and similar analytics tools to understand how visitors use our website. This may include:

  • Total page views and unique visitor counts
  • Device types (desktop, mobile, tablet)
  • Browser types and versions
  • Country-level geographic data (not specific locations)
  • Referral sources (how visitors found our site)
  • Time spent on pages

This information is collected in an aggregated, anonymized format and cannot be used to identify individual users. We use this data solely to improve the website's functionality, user experience, and performance.

2. Server Logs

Like most websites, our web server automatically logs certain information when you visit our site, including:

  • IP addresses (which may be anonymized)
  • Date and time of access
  • Pages visited
  • HTTP status codes
  • Browser user agent strings

These logs are used for security purposes, troubleshooting technical issues, and understanding website traffic patterns. Logs are typically retained for a limited period and then deleted.

3. Contact Information

If you contact us through our contact page or email, we may collect the information you provide, such as your name, email address, and message content. This information is used solely to respond to your inquiry and is not used for marketing purposes unless you explicitly consent.

How Your QR Code Data Works

When you create a QR code using MultiLinkQR, all your information (links, labels, colors, settings, etc.) is processed entirely in your web browser using JavaScript. Here's how it works:

  1. Client-Side Processing: All data you enter is processed locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to our servers during the QR code generation process.
  2. URL Encoding: Your settings and links are encoded into a base64 string and embedded directly into the generated URL (for multi-link QR codes).
  3. No Server Storage: We don't store your QR code data on our servers. The only way someone can access your links is if they have the QR code itself or the generated URL.
  4. Direct Access: Anyone who scans your QR code or has the URL can decode and access the information you encoded. This is by design, as it allows your QR codes to work without requiring our servers to be online.

Important: Because your data is encoded in the URL, anyone with access to the QR code or URL can see the links you've included. Only include links you're comfortable sharing publicly. Never encode sensitive information like passwords, personal data, or private content in your QR codes.

Third-Party Services

MultiLinkQR uses the following third-party services that may collect information:

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to understand website usage. Google's privacy policy applies to this service. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking using browser extensions or Google's opt-out tools.
  • Google AdSense: We may display advertisements through Google AdSense. Google uses cookies and similar technologies to serve ads based on your interests and browsing behavior. You can manage ad preferences in your Google account settings.
  • Content Delivery Networks (CDNs): We use CDNs to deliver Bootstrap, JavaScript libraries, and other resources. These services may collect standard web analytics data.

When you click on links in your QR codes (such as YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, or any external website), those third-party services have their own privacy policies that govern how they collect and use your information. We recommend reviewing the privacy practices of any services you link to in your QR codes.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We may use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to:

  • Remember your theme preferences (dark mode/light mode)
  • Save your QR code generator form state locally in your browser
  • Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
  • Serve relevant advertisements (through Google AdSense)

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can set your browser to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. However, disabling cookies may affect some functionality of our website, such as saving your preferences.

We use both session cookies (which expire when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (which remain on your device until deleted or expired). The data stored in cookies is used solely for the purposes described above.

Data Security

  • We use HTTPS encryption to protect data in transit
  • We follow industry-standard security practices
  • We regularly update our software and dependencies
  • We limit access to server logs and analytics data to authorized personnel only

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.
