How to Get Your YouTube Channel Link

YouTube channels have two types of URLs. Here's how to find yours:

Method 1: Custom Channel URL (Preferred)

If you have 100+ subscribers and a custom URL:

  1. Go to YouTube Studio
  2. Click "Customization" ‚Üí "Basic Info"
  3. Look for "Channel URL"
  4. Copy: https://youtube.com/@YourChannelName

Method 2: Channel ID (Everyone Has This)

  1. Visit your YouTube channel
  2. Click on your profile picture ‚Üí "Your Channel"
  3. Copy the URL from browser: https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC...

Method 3: Username URL (Legacy)

Older channels: https://www.youtube.com/user/YourUsername

Pro Tip: Custom URLs (@YourName) are cleaner and easier to remember. Apply for one in YouTube Studio once you meet requirements.

YouTube QR Code Design Best Practices

Color Schemes That Work

  • YouTube Red (#FF0000): Instant brand recognition
  • Black & Red: Bold, professional contrast
  • Your Brand Colors: Match your channel branding
  • High Contrast Always: Dark QR on light background

Logo Integration Tips

  • Use your YouTube channel profile picture
  • Keep logo under 20% of total QR area
  • Circular crop works best
  • Add white border around logo for separation
  • Test scanning extensively after adding logo

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong URL Type: Using a specific video URL when you want channel subscriptions
  • QR Too Small: Must be at least 1 inch for reliable scanning
  • No Call-to-Action: Always add text: "Scan to Subscribe" or "Watch on YouTube"
  • Poor Contrast: Light QR codes don't scan well
  • Not Testing: Test with multiple phone types before mass printing
  • Forgetting Tracking: Always use UTM parameters to measure success
  • Linking to Unlisted/Private Videos: Ensure linked content is publicly accessible

Legal & Compliance

  • YouTube Guidelines: Comply with YouTube Community Guidelines and Terms of Service
  • Age Restrictions: Clearly mark if content is 18+
  • Copyright: Only promote content you have rights to
  • FTC Disclosures: If promoting sponsored content, follow FTC guidelines
  • Contest Rules: If running contests via QR codes, clearly state rules and eligibility
