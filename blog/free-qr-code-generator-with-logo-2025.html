Logo Design Best Practices for QR Codes

Not all logos work equally well in QR codes. Follow these guidelines:

Size & Proportion Guidelines

  • Maximum Size: Keep logo under 20-25% of total QR code area
  • Minimum Size: At least 10% for visibility
  • Sweet Spot: 15-18% provides best balance of visibility and scanability
  • Buffer Zone: Maintain clear space around logo for error correction

Logo Shapes That Work Best

  • Circular/Round: Best option‚Äîfits naturally in QR code center
  • Square: Works well if kept small enough
  • Shield/Badge: Excellent for professional/institutional brands
  • Horizontal/Wide: Avoid‚Äîcovers too much QR pattern
  • Vertical/Tall: Avoid‚Äîsimilar scanning issues

Color Considerations

  • High Contrast: Logo should stand out against QR background
  • White Background/Border: Add white padding around logo for separation
  • Brand Colors: Use official brand colors for consistency
  • Avoid Transparency Issues: Ensure logo doesn't blend into QR pattern

Detail & Complexity

  • Simple is Better: Bold, simple logos scan more reliably
  • Avoid Fine Details: Thin lines and small text may not reproduce well
  • High Contrast Elements: Ensure all logo parts are clearly visible
  • Test Readability: Logo should be recognizable at small sizes

Common Logo Placement Mistakes

  • Logo Too Large: Exceeding 25% coverage prevents scanning
  • Complex Logos: Intricate designs with fine details fail at small sizes
  • Low Contrast: Logo blends into QR pattern
  • Wrong Shape: Wide horizontal logos cover critical scanning areas
  • No Background: Logo needs white/light background for separation
  • Poor Quality Image: Low-resolution logos look pixelated and unprofessional
  • Not Testing: Failing to verify scanning before mass printing

QR codes use error correction to remain scannable even with partial obstruction. Understanding this helps optimize logo placement:
