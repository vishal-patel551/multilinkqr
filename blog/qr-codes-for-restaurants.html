QR codes have become essential tools for restaurants, offering contactless menu access, streamlined ordering, and enhanced customer experience. This guide covers everything restaurants need to know about implementing QR codes effectively.

Key Uses of QR Codes in Restaurants

Digital Menus

QR code menus eliminate the need for physical menus, reducing printing costs and making updates easy. Customers scan a QR code on their table to view the menu on their phones. This is especially valuable for hygiene and frequent menu changes.

Online Ordering

QR codes can link directly to online ordering systems, allowing customers to place orders without downloading apps or creating accounts. This streamlines the ordering process and increases convenience.

Reservations and Waitlists

Link QR codes to reservation systems or waitlist platforms, making it easy for customers to book tables or join waitlists directly from their phones.

Feedback and Reviews

QR codes can direct customers to review platforms, feedback forms, or loyalty programs, helping restaurants gather valuable customer insights and build online reputation.

Best Practices for Restaurant QR Codes

  • Place QR codes prominently on tables, at entrances, or on table tents
  • Make QR codes large enough (minimum 2x2 inches) for easy scanning
  • Include clear instructions like "Scan for Menu" or "Scan to Order"
  • Ensure menu pages are mobile-optimized and load quickly
  • Test QR codes regularly to ensure they work correctly
  • Consider multi-link QR codes for menus, ordering, reservations, and social media

Single-Link vs Multi-Link for Restaurants

For restaurants, multi-link QR codes are often ideal. With MultiLinkQR, you can create one QR code that opens your menu, online ordering system, reservation page, and social media profiles. This gives customers all the information they need in one scan.

Single-link QR codes work well for focused purposes, such as linking directly to your menu or ordering system. Choose based on your needs: multi-link for comprehensive access, single-link for specific functions.
